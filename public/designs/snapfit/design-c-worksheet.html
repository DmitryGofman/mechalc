STEP 1 — GEOMETRY

The arm bends as a cantilever when the hook rides over the mating edge. Strain grows with thickness and undercut, and falls with the square of length — length is your strongest lever.

R/t below ~0.5 concentrates strain at the root.

STEP 3 — RAMPS & FRICTION

The entry ramp turns your push into beam deflection; friction taxes it. Past μ·tanα = 1 the wedge model self-locks: no finite push force will assemble the joint.

90° = permanent (non-releasable) joint.

STEP 5 — WHAT DOMINATES THE RESULT

Each bar shows how a +10 % change in one input moves the strain utilization U (left) and the insertion force W (right). Note that the modulus moves force but not strain — in a displacement-controlled snap, strain is pure geometry.

Model: linear Euler–Bernoulli cantilever, ideally fixed root, secant modulus, Coulomb friction (BASF & Bayer snap-fit manuals; constants ÷4 / ÷6.528 / ÷5.136 verified at runtime against an independent Castigliano integral: —). Outside its envelope — short beams, large deflection, creep, fatigue, weld lines — the verdict reads indeterminate rather than green. A pass is a preliminary design check, not a production release.
